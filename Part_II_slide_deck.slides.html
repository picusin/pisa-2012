Investigation Overview¶

For this presentation, I will first show the correlation between the scores obtained by students in each of the 3 disciplines. We'll see this through a series of 3 scatterplots comparing disciplines two by two.

Afterwards, I'll focus on the relationship between each of the 4 background independent variables with the scores, using the violin and box plots obtained in the exploratory visualization.

I changed the layout for the faceted plots having 4 subfigures, so that they're shown on a 2x2 grid and appear larger on the screen. I also changed the figure size for other plots.

Dataset Overview¶

PISA is a survey that examines students from compulsory education on how well prepared they are for life after school. This investigation focuses on a selection of variables from the PISA Survey 2012, with data belonging to around 500K students from 65 different countries.

Correlation between the 3 score variables¶

The 3 score variables show a linear correlation between each other. This correlation is slightly weaker between math and reading scores, as seen by the more disperse pattern of the scatterplot on the left.

Score comparison based on student's gender¶

No clear relationship was found between student's gender and their scores, with the exception of reading, where female students seemed to perform better than male students. This can be observed in the upper right box plot of the illustration below.

Score comparison based on OECD membership¶

Students from OECD countries presented better results in the exams across all 3 disciplines. This could indicate that these countries provide a better background for preparing students for the future. In order to know which factors have a greater impact, the PISA survey could be assessed in more detail. Lots of additional variables are provided in the original dataset.

Score comparison based on parent's education level¶

Students' whose parents had a higher level of education obtained significantly better scores. The violin plot below shows the score distribution based on the mother's education level. While students whose mother had not completed primary school had a median score of around 1200 points, students whose mother had completed upper secondary school presented a median score of around 1500.

Score comparison based on internet use at school¶

The use of internet at school doesn't seem to provide better test results. On the contrary, in non-OECD countries, students who claimed to use internet at school showed slightly lower performance in all 3 disciplines.
